Lesson 7

{{ lesson.subtitle|default:lesson.title }}

Movers operate within clear boundaries. This guide helps Realtors communicate those boundaries—confidently and early.

{{ lesson.meta.duration|default:"10–15 mins" }} {{ lesson.meta.level|default:"All Agents" }} Checklist

Movers work within a clear professional boundary. Realtors who can communicate these rules protect both their clients and themselves from costly mistakes.

The Do’s (Best Practices Realtors Should Encourage Clients To Follow)

Do Pack and Label Electronics Yourself

Movers can move boxes, but they aren’t responsible for how electronics were packed. Labels reduce disputes.

Do Photograph Valuables & Test Appliances

“Before” pics/videos protect everyone. If something was already damaged or dead, movers won’t be unfairly blamed.

Do Confirm Insurance & Claims in Advance

Basic coverage is often by weight (e.g., $0.60/lb). For high-value items, recommend full value protection or supplemental insurance.

Do Keep Cash, Jewelry & Irreplaceables With You

Passports, rings, cash—clients carry these personally. Don’t put them in the truck.

Do Prepare the Home for Movers

Clear hallways, secure pets, reserve elevators/loading docks. Fewer surprises → fewer incidents.

Do Tip Movers Fairly (in Cash)

Rule of thumb: 5–10% of the move or $20–$50 per mover, based on complexity.

Do Create a “Day-Of” Essentials Box

Toiletries, meds, chargers, one change of clothes. Don’t make movers dig for it later.

The Don’ts (Requests Clients Must Avoid)

Don’t Ask for Plumbing, Gas, or Electrical Work

Fridge lines, washers, gas dryers, chandeliers = licensed trades only.

Don’t Expect Movers to Move Prohibited Items

Propane, firearms, paint/chemicals, perishable food, live plants, pets.

Don’t Micromanage Every Step

Movers have a system. Hovering slows them and increases risk.

Don’t Assume Specialty Furniture Will Be Reassembled

Pianos, Murphy beds, built-ins may need specialists or extra notice.

Don’t Leave Electronics Unverified

Test TVs/consoles before move day. Avoid unresolvable disputes.

Don’t Pack Restricted Valuables in Movers’ Care

Cash, fine jewelry, collectibles, sensitive documents stay with client.

Don’t Forget Special Needs Communication

Oversized items (safes, pool tables, aquariums) need advance notice.

Don’t Rely on Movers for Storage Unless Prearranged

Not all movers provide storage. Confirm in advance if you’ll need it.

Realtor’s Quick Script for Clients

“Movers move furniture and boxes. Anything involving water, gas, or electricity requires a licensed pro. For valuables, carry them yourself. And let’s confirm insurance before moving day so you’re covered.”

Key Takeaway: The Do’s protect clients from damage and disputes. The Don’ts protect them from liability and expensive mistakes. Deliver this guide upfront to reduce stress and strengthen trust.
