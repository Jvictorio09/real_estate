Lesson 9

{{ lesson.subtitle|default:lesson.title }}

Knowledge is nice. Saying the right words under pressure? That’s the superpower. Practice the responses here.

{{ lesson.meta.duration|default:"12–18 mins" }} {{ lesson.meta.level|default:"All Agents" }} Scenarios & Activities

Realtors need more than knowledge—they need the ability to apply it in the moment when a stressed client makes an unexpected request. These practice scenarios are designed to simulate those conversations and decision points.

Scenario 1: The Fridge Water Line

Client Request: “Since the movers are already here, can they disconnect the fridge water line? It’s just a quick thing.”

Correct Realtor Response: “Movers aren’t licensed plumbers, so if they disconnect it and there’s a leak, it won’t be covered by insurance. Let’s have a plumber handle that instead.”

Learning Point: Even the simplest appliance connections involve liability.

Scenario 2: The Chandelier

Client Request: “Can the movers take down the chandelier and put it up in the new house?”

Correct Realtor Response: “That involves electrical wiring, so movers can’t legally handle it. Let’s arrange an electrician so it’s done safely.”

Learning Point: Electrical tasks must always be performed by licensed professionals.

Scenario 3: The Plant Move

Client Request: “I don’t want to leave these plants behind—can the movers take them across the state line?”

Correct Realtor Response: “Unfortunately, movers aren’t allowed to transport plants across states because of agricultural restrictions. I can suggest alternatives for transporting them.”

Learning Point: Not all items are legal to transport, even if clients believe it’s harmless.

Scenario 4: The Flat-Screen TV

Client Situation: After the move, a client says: “My TV isn’t working. The movers must have broken it.”

Correct Realtor Guidance: “Did you test the TV before the move? Movers are responsible for items packed and transported, but electronics need to be checked before moving day to avoid disputes.”

Learning Point: Testing electronics beforehand prevents false blame and protects both movers and clients.

Checklist Activity

From this list, mark whether each is a “Mover Task” or a “Licensed Pro Task”. One item is “Client Only”.

Disassemble a bed frame

Disconnect a gas dryer

Pack kitchen glassware

Remove a chandelier

Transport jewelry and cash

Wrap and move a sofa

Connect a washing machine

Move boxes into a storage unit

Score: 0/8
Static Answer Key (for print)
  • Bed frame – Mover
  • Gas dryer – Licensed Pro
  • Kitchen glassware – Mover
  • Chandelier – Licensed Pro
  • Jewelry and cash – Client only (not mover)
  • Sofa – Mover
  • Washing machine – Licensed Pro
  • Storage unit – Mover

Role-Play Exercise

Pair Realtors together. One plays the client, one plays the Realtor. The “client” makes escalating requests (disconnecting appliances, moving plants, handling valuables). The Realtor must respond using:

  • The Do’s & Don’ts Guide
  • At least one real-world story to reinforce their boundary
  • A professional script to protect both sides

Debrief: After the exercise, share which parts felt natural and which responses need more practice.

Key Takeaway: Practice scenarios prepare Realtors to respond calmly and confidently in high-stress moments—turning boundaries into professional guidance instead of conflict.
