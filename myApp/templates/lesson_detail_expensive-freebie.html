The “While You’re Here” Problem

The most common—and dangerous—words Realtors hear from clients:

“While you’re here, could you just…”

  • Disconnect the fridge line
  • Take down a chandelier
  • Move my plants across state lines
  • Remove doors and rehang them
  • Hook up the gas dryer

What seems like a harmless favor often leads to liability disasters.

Real Costs of Free Favors

  • Flooded Kitchen: A fridge line disconnected incorrectly = $50,000+ in water damage.
  • Gas Leak: A dryer hookup by movers = fire hazard and potential lawsuits.
  • Illegal Transport: Plants moved across state borders = agricultural fines + refusal of claim.
  • Insurance Denials: If movers perform unlicensed work, insurance providers can void coverage.

Why Clients Ask for Freebies

  • Stress + urgency: They just want the job done.
  • Assumption of skill: “Movers look handy, they can probably handle it.”
  • Convenience mindset: “Why call a pro when movers are already here?”

How Realtors Should Respond

Use a firm but friendly script:

“Movers are fantastic at moving, but for anything involving plumbing, gas, or electricity, it’s safer—and often required by law—to bring in a licensed professional.”

“I want to protect you from an expensive accident. Let’s have the right specialist handle that.”

Key Takeaway: A small “freebie” can quickly become the most expensive mistake of the move. Realtors must train clients to separate moving tasks from trade work.
