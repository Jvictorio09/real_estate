Surface the unspoken assumptions, defuse the emotions, and set clean expectations before moving day.

{{ lesson.meta.duration|default:"8–12 mins" }} {{ lesson.meta.level|default:"All Agents" }} Client Psychology

Common Realtor Assumptions

  • “Movers can handle it.”

    Many Realtors assume movers are “handy” and can tackle any household task. In reality, movers are trained in logistics, lifting, and packing—not licensed trades like plumbing, gas fitting, or electrical.

  • “Clients know what movers do.”

    Realtors sometimes believe clients understand the limits. But most clients think movers are “all-purpose helpers.” This disconnect sets the stage for unrealistic requests.

Common Client Assumptions

  • “While you’re here, could you just…?”

    Clients often think asking movers for a small favor saves time. They don’t realize one shortcut can turn into five- or six-figure damages.

  • “Movers are responsible for everything.”

    Clients believe movers should cover any breakage—even when the client failed to test electronics or pack valuables correctly.

  • “Insurance will handle it.”

    Clients assume all damages are automatically covered. In truth, mover liability is limited, and claims can be denied if movers performed tasks outside their legal scope.

Emotions That Surface on Moving Day

  1. Stress — Moving is ranked as one of life’s most stressful events. Clients are overwhelmed and more likely to make rash, last-minute requests.
  2. Frustration — If something goes wrong, blame is often misdirected—toward movers or Realtors.
  3. Urgency — Clients feel pressed for time and push movers to “just do it quickly,” ignoring legal or safety risks.
  4. Relief-Seeking — Clients want everything handled, so they assume movers can “fix” any issue on the spot.

Realtor’s Role in Addressing These Emotions

  • Acknowledge client stress. “I know moving is overwhelming—let’s make sure nothing adds unnecessary risk.”
  • Reframe expectations. “Movers are excellent at transport, but for anything involving water, gas, or electricity, we’ll bring in licensed pros.”
  • Prevent frustration through education. Share stories before moving day, so clients understand the risks.
  • Build trust by setting boundaries early. Clients respect Realtors who protect them, even if it means saying “no.”

Key Point: Misconceptions + stress = bad decisions. A Realtor who can anticipate these assumptions and emotions is far less likely to be blamed when problems arise.
